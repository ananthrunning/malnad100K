What he is attempting

A 100-kilometre mountain run through the Western Ghats

The Malnad Ultra 100K is India's largest trail ultramarathon, held in the coffee estates and forest trails of Karnataka's Malnad region. The course covers 100 kilometres across two identical 50-kilometre loops, gaining and losing 3,410 metres of elevation in total — the equivalent of climbing from sea level to the summit of a significant mountain, twice.

The race starts at 6:30 AM on November 28, 2026. Runners have until 3:30 AM on November 29 to finish — a 21-hour window. There are strict intermediate cutoffs: the first 50 km must be completed by 4:30 PM, and runners must reach the 88 km checkpoint by 1:30 AM. The second loop runs entirely in darkness, requiring a mandatory headlamp.

Ananth completed the Malnad 50K in November 2025, earning his qualification for the 100K. This campaign — 32 weeks, three phases, five races — is the methodical preparation for what comes next.

The only finishing time that matters for a first 100K is the one that says "Finisher." This campaign is about crossing that line.

The race itself · November 28, 2026

What happens on race day

The strategy for a first 100-kilometre race is simple and requires everything to execute: run the first 50 kilometres conservatively enough that the second 50 kilometres are possible. Most people who don't finish don't fail because they were unfit — they fail because they ran the first loop too fast.

The plan is to complete Loop 1 in approximately 9 to 9.5 hours, reaching the halfway point at around 3:30–4:00 PM with reserves intact. Loop 2 begins in the late afternoon and runs deep into the night — a mandatory headlamp is required after dark. The race cutoff is 3:30 AM, providing a generous buffer.

Goal A: Sub-17 hours. Goal B: 17–19 hours. Goal C: Finish. The September Bengaluru Marathon will determine which goal is realistic. The one number that matters more than finish time is the Loop 1 split — arrive at 50 km in 9–9.5 hours, with your legs still feeling like legs, and you will finish.
